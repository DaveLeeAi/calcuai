## What Is Future Value?

Future value (FV) is the projected worth of a current sum of money or series of payments at a specified date in the future, assuming a particular rate of return. It is the cornerstone calculation behind investment planning, retirement projections, and savings goals.

A $25,000 investment earning 8% annually has a future value of $53,973 in 10 years and $116,524 in 20 years — all without additional contributions. Add $300 per month in contributions and the 20-year future value jumps to $293,243. The formula for a lump sum is FV = PV × (1 + r)^n, where PV is present value, r is the periodic rate, and n is the number of periods.

Future value answers the forward-looking question: "What will my money be worth later?" Its counterpart, present value, answers the reverse: "What is a future sum worth today?" Both rely on the time value of money — the principle that a dollar today is worth more than a dollar tomorrow because of its earning potential. A $5,000 annual IRA contribution growing at 8% annually reaches approximately $566,416 after 30 years of consistent investing.
