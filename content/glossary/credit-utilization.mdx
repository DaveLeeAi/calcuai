## What Is Credit Utilization?

Credit utilization is the percentage of your available revolving credit that you're currently using. It's calculated by dividing your total credit card balances by your total credit limits. FICO and VantageScore models weight utilization heavily — it accounts for roughly 30% of your FICO score, making it the second most influential factor after payment history.

A cardholder with $5,000 in balances across cards totaling $20,000 in credit limits has a 25% utilization ratio. Most credit scoring models consider utilization below 30% acceptable, but borrowers with the highest credit scores typically keep utilization under 10%. Crossing the 30% threshold can drop a credit score by 20–50 points even with perfect payment history, because high utilization signals potential overextension to lenders.

Both per-card utilization and overall utilization matter. Maxing out a single card (100% utilization on that card) while keeping others at 0% can still hurt your score, even if your overall utilization is low. The safest strategy is keeping every individual card below 30% and your aggregate ratio below 10%.
