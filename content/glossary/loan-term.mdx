## What Is a Loan Term?

A loan term is the length of time you have to repay a loan in full, typically expressed in months or years. Common terms include 30-year and 15-year mortgages, 60-month auto loans, and 10-year student loans.

The term you choose creates a direct trade-off between monthly payment size and total interest cost. On a $250,000 mortgage at 7%: a 30-year term costs $1,663/month with $348,772 in total interest, while a 15-year term costs $2,247/month but only $154,484 in total interest — saving $194,288. The shorter term costs $584 more per month but cuts total interest nearly in half.

Shorter terms carry higher monthly payments but lower total cost. Longer terms make each payment more affordable but significantly increase the total amount paid. Auto loans typically range from 36 to 84 months; personal loans from 12 to 60 months. Choosing the right term depends on balancing monthly cash flow against long-term cost. Choosing a 15-year term over a 30-year term on a $300,000 mortgage at 6.5% raises the monthly payment from $1,896 to $2,613 but saves over $191,000 in total interest. Shorter terms build equity faster but require higher monthly cash flow.
