## What Is Present Value?

Present value (PV) is the current worth of a future sum of money or stream of payments, discounted at a specific rate of return. It answers the question: "How much is a future payment worth in today's dollars?"

A $50,000 payment due in 10 years, discounted at 6%, has a present value of $27,919. This means you would need to invest $27,919 today at 6% annual return to have exactly $50,000 in 10 years. The formula is PV = FV / (1 + r)^n, where FV is the future value, r is the discount rate per period, and n is the number of periods.

Present value is the foundation of investment analysis, business valuation, and retirement planning. When comparing two investment options — say, $100,000 today versus $150,000 in 8 years — present value calculation reveals which is actually worth more (at 6% discount, the $150,000 future payment has a PV of $94,177, making the $100,000 today the better choice). It is the inverse of future value: while FV projects forward, PV discounts backward.
