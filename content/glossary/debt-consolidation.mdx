## What Is Debt Consolidation?

Debt consolidation combines multiple debts — typically high-interest credit cards — into a single loan with one monthly payment, usually at a lower interest rate. The goal is to reduce total interest paid and simplify repayment by replacing several creditors with one.

A borrower with three credit cards totaling $25,000 at an average 18% APR paying $750/month would pay approximately $11,300 in interest over 47 months. Consolidating into a 5-year personal loan at 8% reduces the monthly payment to $507 and total interest to $5,415 — saving $5,885 and freeing up $243/month. The tradeoff is a longer repayment timeline (60 months vs. 47), but the lower rate more than compensates.

Common consolidation methods include personal loans (fixed rate, fixed term), balance transfer credit cards (0% intro APR for 12–21 months, then variable rate), and home equity loans (lowest rates but secured by your home). The right option depends on the debt amount, your credit score, and whether you can pay off a balance transfer before the promotional period ends. Consolidation only works if you stop accumulating new debt on the freed-up credit cards.
