## What Is Equity?

Equity is the difference between the current market value of an asset and the amount still owed on it. In homeownership, equity equals your home's value minus your remaining mortgage balance. In business and investing, equity represents an ownership stake.

If your home is worth $400,000 and you owe $260,000 on the mortgage, you have $140,000 in home equity — or 35% equity. Equity grows in two ways: by paying down your mortgage principal and by home value appreciation. At 3% annual appreciation, a $400,000 home gains $12,000 in equity from appreciation alone in the first year.

Home equity matters because it can be borrowed against (via a HELOC or home equity loan), it determines how much you net when selling, and it eliminates PMI once you reach 20% equity. Negative equity — owing more than the home is worth — can occur if property values decline sharply. On the investment side, equity in a company (stock) represents your proportional ownership claim on its assets and earnings. If your home is worth $450,000 and you owe $280,000, you have $170,000 in equity — roughly 38% of the property's value.
