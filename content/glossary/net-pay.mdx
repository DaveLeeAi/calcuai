## What Is Net Pay?

Net pay (also called take-home pay) is the amount you actually receive after all deductions are subtracted from your gross pay. Deductions include federal income tax, state income tax, Social Security (6.2%), Medicare (1.45%), retirement contributions, and health insurance premiums.

A worker earning $5,000 gross per biweekly paycheck in the 22% federal bracket with 5% state tax, 6% 401(k) contribution, and $200 health insurance premium takes home approximately $3,525 — only 70.5% of gross pay. The gap between gross and net surprises many workers, especially those entering the workforce for the first time.

The distinction matters for budgeting: your rent, groceries, and savings all come from net pay, not gross. When evaluating a job offer, convert the salary to an estimated net figure using your filing status and state tax rate before committing to fixed expenses.
