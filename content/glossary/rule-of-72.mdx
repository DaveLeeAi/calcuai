## What Is the Rule of 72?

The Rule of 72 is a mental math shortcut that estimates how many years it takes for an investment to double at a given annual return rate. Divide 72 by the annual interest rate to get the approximate doubling time: at 8% annual return, money doubles in roughly 72 ÷ 8 = 9 years.

The rule works because it approximates the exact formula — ln(2) / ln(1 + r) — with surprising accuracy for rates between 2% and 15%. At 6%, the Rule of 72 predicts 12.0 years; the exact answer is 11.9 years. At 10%, the rule says 7.2 years; the exact answer is 7.27 years. The approximation breaks down at very high rates (above 20%) but remains reliable for typical investment and savings returns.

The rule also works in reverse: if you want your money to double in 10 years, you need a return of roughly 72 ÷ 10 = 7.2% per year. This makes it a powerful tool for quick retirement planning, comparing investment options, and understanding the real impact of fees — a 1% annual fee effectively doubles the time it takes your money to double. Originally attributed to Luca Pacioli in 1494, the Rule of 72 remains one of the most useful shortcuts in personal finance.
