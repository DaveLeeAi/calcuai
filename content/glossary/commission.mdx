## What Is Commission?

Commission is compensation paid to an employee or agent based on a percentage of the sales or transactions they generate. It is most common in sales, real estate, financial services, and recruiting, and can be the sole form of pay or combined with a base salary.

A real estate agent earning a 3% commission on a $400,000 home sale receives $12,000. A sales representative with a $50,000 base salary plus 8% commission who generates $300,000 in annual sales earns $50,000 + $24,000 = $74,000 total compensation. Commission structures vary widely: flat rate (fixed percentage on all sales), tiered (higher percentages above thresholds), residual (ongoing payments from recurring revenue), and draw against commission (advance that must be earned back).

Commission income creates unique financial planning challenges. It is taxable as regular income and subject to the same withholding rules as salary. Budgeting is harder because monthly income fluctuates. Lenders typically require a 2-year history of commission earnings to count it toward mortgage qualification, averaging the amount to smooth variability. A salesperson earning 8% commission on a $50,000 deal takes home $4,000 from that single transaction.
