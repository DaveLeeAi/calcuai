## What Is BMI?

BMI (Body Mass Index) is a numerical value calculated from a person's weight and height, used as a screening tool to categorize weight status. The formula is BMI = weight (kg) / height (m)². In imperial units: BMI = (weight in lbs × 703) / (height in inches)².

A 5'10" person weighing 170 pounds has a BMI of 24.4, placing them in the "normal weight" category. The standard adult ranges are: underweight (below 18.5), normal weight (18.5–24.9), overweight (25.0–29.9), and obese (30.0 and above). For the same height, crossing from normal to overweight occurs at about 174 pounds.

BMI is widely used in clinical settings and population health studies because it is quick, free, and requires no special equipment. However, it has well-documented limitations: it does not distinguish between muscle and fat mass, does not account for fat distribution, and may miscategorize muscular athletes as overweight. A bodybuilder with 10% body fat and a sedentary person with 30% body fat can have identical BMIs. For a more complete picture, pair BMI with body fat percentage or waist circumference measurements.
