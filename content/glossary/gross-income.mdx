## What Is Gross Income?

Gross income is the total amount of money you earn before any deductions, taxes, or withholdings are subtracted. For an employee, it is the full salary or hourly wages before federal, state, and payroll taxes. For a business, it is total revenue minus the cost of goods sold.

A worker earning $75,000 gross salary might take home approximately $56,000–$60,000 after federal income tax, state tax, Social Security (6.2%), and Medicare (1.45%) are withheld. The gap between gross and net pay — often 20–30% — is why understanding gross income matters for budgeting, tax planning, and qualifying for loans.

Lenders use gross income to determine borrowing capacity. The standard mortgage qualification rule limits housing costs to 28% of gross monthly income: on a $75,000 salary ($6,250/month gross), that caps your housing payment at about $1,750. Gross income also determines your federal tax bracket and eligibility for deductions and credits. If your salary is $75,000 and you earn $5,000 in freelance income plus $2,000 in investment dividends, your gross income totals $82,000. This pre-deduction figure determines loan eligibility and qualification for programs like Roth IRA contributions, which phase out above $161,000 for single filers in 2024.
