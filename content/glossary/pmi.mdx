## What Is PMI?

PMI (Private Mortgage Insurance) is insurance that protects the lender — not the borrower — when a homebuyer makes a down payment of less than 20% on a conventional mortgage. The borrower pays the premium, but the lender is the beneficiary if the loan defaults.

On a $350,000 home with 10% down ($35,000), PMI typically costs between $100 and $200 per month, depending on credit score and loan-to-value ratio. At $150/month, that is $1,800 per year added to your housing cost — money that builds zero equity. Over the 7–10 years it takes most buyers to reach 20% equity, PMI can total $12,600–$18,000.

PMI is automatically canceled on conventional loans once the loan balance reaches 78% of the original home value (by law under the Homeowners Protection Act). You can also request cancellation at 80% loan-to-value. FHA loans have their own version called MIP (Mortgage Insurance Premium), which often lasts the entire loan term. The fastest way to eliminate PMI is a larger down payment or accelerated principal payments. On a $300,000 loan, PMI typically adds $125–$250 per month until you reach 20% equity.
