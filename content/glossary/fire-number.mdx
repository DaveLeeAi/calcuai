## What Is a FIRE Number?

A FIRE number (Financial Independence, Retire Early) is the portfolio size that generates enough annual investment income to cover your living expenses indefinitely without employment income. It's calculated by dividing your annual expenses by your safe withdrawal rate: someone spending $40,000 per year with a 4% withdrawal rate needs $40,000 ÷ 0.04 = $1,000,000 to reach financial independence.

The concept originates from the 1992 book *Your Money or Your Life* by Vicki Robin and Joe Dominguez, and the withdrawal rate framework comes from the 1998 Trinity Study, which found that a 4% initial withdrawal rate (adjusted for inflation) survived 30-year retirement periods in 95% of historical market scenarios. FIRE practitioners aim to reach their number by maximizing their savings rate — someone saving 50% of after-tax income can reach FIRE in roughly 17 years regardless of income level.

Several FIRE variants exist: **Lean FIRE** targets minimal expenses ($25,000–$40,000/year), **Fat FIRE** targets comfortable spending ($100,000+/year), and **Barista FIRE** supplements a smaller portfolio with part-time work. The FIRE number is a planning target, not a guarantee — actual sustainability depends on market returns, inflation, spending flexibility, and healthcare costs.
