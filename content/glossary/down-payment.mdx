## What Is a Down Payment?

A down payment is the upfront cash a buyer pays toward a purchase, reducing the amount that must be financed through a loan. It is most commonly associated with home purchases but applies to auto loans and other large purchases as well.

On a $350,000 home, a 20% down payment equals $70,000, leaving a $280,000 mortgage. A 10% down payment ($35,000) means financing $315,000 — adding roughly $233 to your monthly payment at 7% interest and requiring private mortgage insurance (PMI) until you reach 20% equity. Over 30 years, the smaller down payment costs approximately $48,000 more in interest plus $15,000–$25,000 in PMI premiums.

The 20% threshold is significant because it eliminates PMI on conventional loans, gives you immediate equity, and typically qualifies you for better interest rates. However, FHA loans allow as little as 3.5% down, and VA and USDA loans offer 0% down for eligible buyers. The trade-off is between preserving cash reserves and minimizing long-term borrowing cost. On a $400,000 home, a 20% down payment equals $80,000, while an FHA minimum of 3.5% requires just $14,000 upfront.
