## What Is Compound Interest?

Compound interest is interest calculated on both the initial principal and the accumulated interest from previous periods. Unlike simple interest (which applies only to the original amount), compound interest causes a balance to grow exponentially over time.

At 7% annual return with monthly compounding, $10,000 grows to $19,671.51 in 10 years and $38,696.84 in 20 years — without adding a single extra dollar. The same $10,000 at simple interest would reach only $17,000 in 10 years. That $2,671 gap at year 10 widens to $24,696 by year 20, illustrating why Albert Einstein reportedly called compound interest "the eighth wonder of the world."

The frequency of compounding matters: daily compounding earns slightly more than monthly, which earns more than annual. A savings account advertising 5.00% APY compounded daily yields more per year than one compounded quarterly at the same nominal rate. The formula is A = P(1 + r/n)^(nt), where P is principal, r is the annual rate, n is the compounding frequency, and t is time in years.
