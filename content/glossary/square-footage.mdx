## What Is Square Footage?

Square footage is the measurement of an area in square feet (ft²), calculated by multiplying length by width. It is the standard unit for measuring floor area, wall area, and land area in the United States and is fundamental to construction estimating, real estate pricing, and material calculations.

A room measuring 12 feet by 15 feet has 180 square feet of floor area. For irregular shapes, divide the space into rectangles, calculate each separately, and add them together. A 1,500 sq ft home at $200 per square foot is valued at $300,000 — making price per square foot one of the most common real estate comparison metrics.

Square footage drives material estimates across nearly every construction and home improvement project. Flooring requires area plus 10% waste factor: 180 sq ft of floor needs about 198 sq ft of material. Paint coverage averages 350–400 sq ft per gallon. Drywall sheets cover 32 sq ft each (4×8). Accurate square footage measurement prevents both costly over-ordering and project-delaying shortages. A 2,000-square-foot home at the national median of roughly $155 per square foot translates to a $310,000 property value.
