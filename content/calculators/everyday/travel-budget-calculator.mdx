This calculator captures all seven categories and shows the real number before you book — not the credit card shock when you return.

## How to Use This Calculator

1. **Enter the number of travelers** — count every person whose expenses you're covering. Children count if they need their own plane seat, meals, and activity tickets. Infants traveling on a lap seat can be excluded.

2. **Enter the number of nights** — this is hotel nights, not travel days. A Monday-to-Friday trip is 4 nights. The calculator automatically adds 1 to get the number of days (you eat and take transport on your departure day too).

3. **Enter flight or transport cost** — this is the total round-trip cost for all travelers combined. If flying 2 people at $400 each, enter $800. For road trips, enter total fuel cost instead. Include baggage fees if applicable.

4. **Set accommodation per night** — enter the per-room or per-unit nightly rate. If you're booking two hotel rooms at $150 each, enter $300. Check Booking.com, Hotels.com, or Airbnb for rates at your destination.

5. **Set daily budgets** — food budget is per person per day (restaurants, groceries, coffee, snacks). Activities budget is per person per day (tours, museums, attractions). Local transport is per day for the whole group (metro passes, taxis, rideshare). Shopping and insurance are flat totals for the entire trip.

## The Formula

**Accommodation total:**

$$\text{Accommodation} = \text{Nights} \times \text{Rate Per Night}$$

**Food total (days = nights + 1):**

$$\text{Food} = (\text{Nights} + 1) \times \text{Daily Food Budget} \times \text{Travelers}$$

**Activities total:**

$$\text{Activities} = (\text{Nights} + 1) \times \text{Daily Activity Budget} \times \text{Travelers}$$

**Local transport total:**

$$\text{Transport} = (\text{Nights} + 1) \times \text{Daily Transport Budget}$$

**Total trip cost:**

$$\text{Total} = \text{Flights} + \text{Accommodation} + \text{Food} + \text{Activities} + \text{Transport} + \text{Shopping} + \text{Insurance}$$

**Per-person and per-day breakdowns:**

$$\text{Cost/Person} = \frac{\text{Total}}{\text{Travelers}} \qquad \text{Cost/Day} = \frac{\text{Total}}{\text{Nights} + 1}$$

Where:

- **Nights** = number of hotel nights (days = nights + 1)
- **Daily Food Budget** = average food spending per person per day
- **Daily Activity Budget** = average activity spending per person per day
- **Daily Transport Budget** = local transport cost per day for the group
- **Flights**, **Shopping**, and **Insurance** are flat totals for the entire trip

**Source:** Bureau of Labor Statistics — Consumer Expenditure Survey (2024); U.S. Travel Association — Travel Price Index; Numbeo — Cost of Living Index.

## Key Factors That Affect Your Travel Budget

**Destination cost of living** is the single biggest variable. A $60/day food budget covers casual dining in Lisbon or Bangkok but barely covers two meals in London, Tokyo, or Zurich. Numbeo's Cost of Living Index ranks cities relative to New York City (index 100): Lisbon scores 42, Bangkok 34, London 75, and Tokyo 68. Scale your daily budgets proportionally.

**Travel season** swings accommodation prices 30–60%. Peak-season hotel rates in Barcelona (June–August) average $180–$250/night versus $100–$140 in November–March. Flights follow similar patterns: booking 2–3 months ahead during shoulder season (April–May, September–October) typically saves 20–35% on both flights and hotels.

**Accommodation type** dramatically shifts the budget. Hotels average $120–$200/night in major cities; Airbnb units run $80–$150 for comparable locations; hostels cost $25–$60. For groups of 3+, a shared Airbnb is almost always the cheapest option per person.

**Group size** reduces per-person cost because flights and accommodation are fixed. Adding a third traveler to a $3,920 two-person trip increases the total by only $1,600 (food + activities) but drops the per-person cost from $1,960 to $1,840.

**Trip duration** affects the cost structure. Short trips (2–3 nights) are flight-dominated — airfare may represent 40–50% of total cost. Longer trips (7+ nights) are accommodation- and food-dominated, with flights dropping to 15–25% of the total.

## Assumptions and Limitations

- **Days = nights + 1** — the calculator assumes you spend money on arrival day. If your flight arrives late at night or you depart early in the morning, actual spending days may differ.
- **Flat daily rates** — food and activity budgets assume the same spending each day. Real travel spending varies: city days cost more than beach or transit days.
- **Accommodation is per-room/unit** — enter the total nightly cost for your accommodation, not per-person. If you need two hotel rooms, double the nightly rate.
- **Flights are total** — the flight input is the combined cost for all travelers, not per-person.
- **Local transport is group-level** — taxi fares, metro passes, and rideshare costs are entered for the whole group per day.
- **No currency conversion** — all values are in USD. Use a currency converter if budgeting in another currency and convert to USD before entering.
- **No contingency buffer** — most financial planners recommend adding 10–15% to any travel budget for unexpected expenses (lost items, medical, missed connections).

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How much does a 7-day vacation cost for 2 people?

A 7-night vacation for 2 people costs $2,500–$5,500 depending on destination and spending level. Budget travelers (hostels, street food, free activities) can manage $2,000–$3,000. Mid-range travelers (3-star hotels, casual dining, paid tours) typically spend $3,500–$5,000. Luxury travelers (4-star+ hotels, fine dining, private tours) should budget $6,000–$10,000+.

### What percentage of a travel budget goes to accommodation?

Accommodation typically represents 25–35% of a total travel budget for trips of 5+ nights. On shorter trips (2–3 nights), flights dominate at 35–45% while accommodation drops to 20–25%. The BLS Consumer Expenditure Survey reports lodging as the largest single travel expense category, averaging 31% of vacation spending.

### How much should I budget for food while traveling?

Budget $40–$70 per person per day for moderate eating in most destinations. This covers breakfast ($8–$15), lunch ($12–$20), and dinner ($20–$35) at casual restaurants. Budget travelers can get by on $20–$35/day using grocery stores, street food, and one restaurant meal. In expensive cities like London, Tokyo, or New York, plan for $70–$100/day.

### Should I buy travel insurance?

Travel insurance costs 4–8% of total trip cost and is worth it for international trips over $3,000, trips with non-refundable bookings, or travel to destinations with expensive healthcare. A $4,000 trip typically costs $160–$320 to insure. Skip it for cheap domestic weekend trips where your health insurance already covers you.

### How do I budget for activities and excursions?

Plan $25–$50 per person per day for activities in most destinations. Museums average $15–$25, guided tours $40–$80, adventure activities $50–$150. Many cities offer free walking tours, free museum days, and no-cost attractions (parks, beaches, markets). Research your destination's top activities and price them individually, then divide by trip days for a daily average.

### Is it cheaper to travel as a group?

Yes — group travel reduces per-person cost by 20–40% compared to solo or couple travel. The savings come from splitting fixed costs: accommodation (one Airbnb vs. multiple hotel rooms), car rentals, taxis, and group activity discounts. A $3,920 trip for 2 ($1,960/person) becomes approximately $5,120 for 4 ($1,280/person) because only food and activity costs scale with group size.

### How far in advance should I book to save money?

Book flights 2–3 months ahead for domestic trips and 3–5 months ahead for international trips. Booking too early (6+ months) or too late (under 3 weeks) typically costs 15–30% more. Hotels offer the best rates 3–6 weeks before your stay. Airbnb hosts often discount listings that haven't been booked within 2 weeks of the date. Use price tracking tools like Google Flights and Hopper to monitor fare trends.

### What's a good daily travel budget for Europe?

Budget $100–$150/person/day for a comfortable European trip covering accommodation, food, transport, and activities. Budget travelers can manage $50–$80/day (hostels, cooking, free attractions). Mid-range travelers should plan $120–$180/day. Western Europe (France, UK, Scandinavia) costs 30–50% more than Southern or Eastern Europe (Portugal, Greece, Poland).
