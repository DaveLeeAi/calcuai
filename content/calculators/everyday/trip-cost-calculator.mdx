## What Your Trip Cost Estimate Shows

Trip budgeting fails when people only plan for gas and hotels. Food, activities, tolls, parking, and impulse purchases quietly add 30–50% to the total cost. This calculator captures all five major expense categories and shows you the real number before you leave — not the surprise total when you get home.

**Total trip cost** is the sum of all expenses: fuel, lodging, food, activities, and miscellaneous. For a typical 3-night family road trip of 600 miles, expect $1,000–$1,500 total. Weekend getaways (1 night, 200 miles) typically run $300–$500 for two people.

**Fuel cost** uses the same formula as a dedicated fuel calculator: distance divided by MPG, multiplied by price per gallon. A 600-mile round trip at 28 MPG and $3.50/gallon costs $75. This is often the smallest expense category for multi-day trips — lodging and food dwarf fuel costs on trips longer than 2 nights.

**Food cost** is calculated per person per meal per day. The number of days equals nights plus one (you eat on travel days too). At $15/meal with 3 meals/day for 2 travelers over 4 days, food totals $360 — often the second-largest expense after lodging. To reduce this, pack breakfasts and snacks (drops the effective meals/day to 1.5–2).

**Cost per person** divides the total evenly. Lodging and fuel don't scale with group size, so adding travelers dramatically reduces per-person cost. A $1,105 trip for 2 people is $552.50 each; the same trip for 4 people is $393.75 each (only food increases with headcount).

**Cost per day** helps compare trip options. A 3-night trip at $276/day vs. a 5-night trip at $200/day shows that longer trips often have lower daily costs because fuel is a one-time expense spread over more days.

## How to Use This Calculator

1. **Enter your round-trip distance** — use Google Maps or your preferred navigation app to get the driving distance. Remember to double it for a round trip (or enter the already-doubled number). Add 10–15% for detours, wrong turns, and side trips if your route isn't highway-direct.

2. **Set fuel inputs** — enter your vehicle's real-world MPG (check your dashboard trip computer, not the EPA sticker — real-world is typically 15–20% lower) and the current gas price in your area. Use GasBuddy to find prices along your route.

3. **Enter lodging details** — number of nights and average per-night cost. Check hotel booking sites for your destination. Budget hotels average $80–$120/night, mid-range $120–$200, and upscale $200+. Campgrounds run $25–$50/night. Airbnb can be cheaper for groups splitting one rental.

4. **Set food and activity budgets** — average meal cost per person and meals per day. Fast food averages $10–$12/meal, casual dining $15–$25, and restaurants $25–$50+. Activities budget covers tickets, tours, park passes, and entertainment for the entire trip. Miscellaneous covers tolls, parking, souvenirs, and tips.

## The Formula

**Fuel cost:**

$$\text{Fuel} = \frac{\text{Distance}}{\text{MPG}} \times \text{Fuel Price}$$

**Lodging cost:**

$$\text{Lodging} = \text{Nights} \times \text{Hotel Per Night}$$

**Food cost (days = nights + 1):**

$$\text{Food} = \text{Meals/Day} \times \text{Meal Cost} \times \text{Travelers} \times (\text{Nights} + 1)$$

**Total trip cost:**

$$\text{Total} = \text{Fuel} + \text{Lodging} + \text{Food} + \text{Activities} + \text{Misc}$$

**Per-person and per-day breakdowns:**

$$\text{Cost/Person} = \frac{\text{Total}}{\text{Travelers}} \qquad \text{Cost/Day} = \frac{\text{Total}}{\text{Nights} + 1}$$

Where:

- **Distance** = round-trip driving distance in miles
- **MPG** = vehicle fuel efficiency (miles per gallon)
- **Meal Cost** = average cost per person per meal
- **Activities** and **Misc** = total budgets for the entire trip (not per-person)

**Source:** AAA — Your Driving Costs (2024); Bureau of Transportation Statistics; GSA Per Diem Rates.

## Worked Example

### 3-Night Beach Trip — 600 Miles Round Trip for 2 Travelers

A couple is planning a 3-night beach trip, driving 300 miles each way in a sedan that gets 28 MPG.

**Inputs:**
- Distance: 600 miles (round trip)
- Fuel: 28 MPG, $3.50/gallon
- Lodging: 3 nights at $150/night
- Meals: 3/day at $15/person
- Travelers: 2
- Activities: $200 total
- Miscellaneous: $100 total

**Step 1 — Fuel cost:**

Fuel = 600 / 28 × $3.50 = **$75.00**

**Step 2 — Lodging:**

Lodging = 3 × $150 = **$450.00**

**Step 3 — Food (4 days = 3 nights + 1):**

Food = 3 × $15 × 2 × 4 = **$360.00**

**Step 4 — Total:**

Total = $75 + $450 + $360 + $200 + $100 = **$1,185.00**

Cost per person = $1,185 / 2 = **$592.50**

Cost per day = $1,185 / 4 = **$296.25**

Food and lodging account for 68% of the total cost. Cutting meal costs to $10/person (fast food and packed lunches) would save $120, dropping the total to $1,065.

## Key Factors That Affect Trip Cost

**Lodging** is typically the largest expense for multi-day trips. A 3-night stay at $200/night costs more than fuel for a 1,200-mile round trip. Booking strategies: reserve 2–3 months ahead for 15–30% savings, consider Sunday–Thursday rates (often 30–40% cheaper), and compare hotels against Airbnb for groups of 3+.

**Group size** dramatically changes per-person cost. Fuel and lodging don't increase with more travelers, so the per-person cost drops roughly 25–35% for each additional person. A $1,185 trip costs $592 for 2 people, $395 for 3, and $296 for 4.

**Trip duration** affects the cost structure. Day trips are fuel-dominated. Multi-day trips are lodging- and food-dominated. The "sweet spot" for per-day value is typically 4–5 nights, where the fixed fuel cost is spread across more days.

**Season and destination** drive hotel prices. Summer beach towns and winter ski resorts command premium rates. Shoulder seasons (April–May, September–October) often offer 20–40% lower lodging costs with better weather than you'd expect.

## Assumptions and Limitations

Fuel cost assumes constant MPG and a single fuel price. Real-world MPG varies with highway vs. city driving, speed, cargo weight, and terrain — mountain driving can reduce MPG by 20–30%. Gas prices vary by state and station. Lodging cost assumes a flat per-night rate; many destinations charge resort fees, parking fees, or cleaning fees not included in the advertised rate. Food cost uses a per-meal average and does not distinguish between breakfast ($8–$12), lunch ($12–$18), and dinner ($18–$35). Activities and miscellaneous are user-estimated totals. Tolls are included in miscellaneous but not calculated from the route. The calculator does not account for vehicle wear and depreciation (AAA estimates $0.09–$0.12/mile for operating costs beyond fuel).

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How much does a road trip cost per mile?

The fuel cost alone is $0.10–$0.15 per mile for a typical sedan (25–30 MPG at $3.00–$3.50/gallon). Including all expenses (lodging, food, activities), a multi-day road trip averages $0.75–$2.00 per mile depending on trip length, lodging quality, and group size. AAA estimates total driving costs (including depreciation, insurance, and maintenance) at $0.67/mile for a midsize sedan.

### How much should I budget for food on a road trip?

Plan $30–$50 per person per day for a mix of dining out and casual meals. Budget travelers can reduce this to $15–$25/day by packing breakfast, buying groceries, and eating fast food. Families with children should budget closer to $20–$30/person/day. The GSA per diem meal allowance for most U.S. cities is $59/day.

### Is it cheaper to drive or fly?

For 1–2 travelers on trips over 500 miles one-way, flying is often cheaper when you factor in hotel nights, food, and time. For 3–4 travelers on trips under 500 miles, driving is almost always cheaper because flight costs scale per-person while gas doesn't. The breakeven point is typically 400–600 miles one-way for 2 travelers.

### How can I reduce road trip costs?

The highest-impact savings: (1) pack breakfast and snacks to cut food costs 30–40%, (2) book lodging 2+ months ahead for 15–30% savings, (3) travel with more people to split fixed costs, (4) drive at 60–65 mph instead of 75+ to improve MPG by 10–15%, and (5) use gas price apps to save $0.10–$0.30/gallon along your route.

### Should I include car maintenance costs in my trip budget?

For budgeting purposes, AAA recommends adding $0.09/mile for maintenance and $0.03/mile for tires on road trips. On a 600-mile trip, that's $54 for maintenance and $18 for tire wear. Most travelers don't include these, but they're real costs — especially for older vehicles or trips involving mountain passes.
