## Your Result Explained

Speed conversions use fixed ratios derived from the definitions of the underlying distance and time units. A mile is exactly 1,609.344 meters, an hour is exactly 3,600 seconds, and a nautical mile is exactly 1,852 meters. These definitions make every speed conversion a simple multiplication or division by a constant factor — no rounding or approximation needed for the conversion itself.

The converter also supports Mach numbers (referenced to the speed of sound in dry air at 20°C: 343 m/s) and fractions of the speed of light (c = 299,792,458 m/s). For physics problems involving speed calculations from distance and time, see the [Velocity Calculator](/calculators/science/velocity-calculator). For converting between distance units, use the [Length Converter](/calculators/conversion/length-converter).

## The Formula

All conversions pass through meters per second (m/s) as the base unit:

$$\text{result} = \text{value} \times \frac{\text{fromFactor}}{\text{toFactor}}$$

## Worked Examples

### Example: Converting Highway Speed Limits

A European highway has a speed limit of 130 km/h. What is this in mph, m/s, and knots?

**Step-by-step:**

1. Convert to m/s (base unit): 130 × 0.277778 = **36.11 m/s**
2. Convert m/s to mph: 36.11 / 0.44704 = **80.78 mph**
3. Convert m/s to ft/s: 36.11 / 0.3048 = **118.47 ft/s**
4. Convert m/s to knots: 36.11 / 0.514444 = **70.19 knots**
5. Convert m/s to Mach: 36.11 / 343 = **0.1053 Mach**

**Result:** 130 km/h = **80.78 mph** = **36.11 m/s** = **118.47 ft/s** = **70.19 knots** = **Mach 0.1053**.

**Practical interpretation:** The 130 km/h European highway limit is roughly equivalent to 80 mph — noticeably faster than the typical 65-70 mph US interstate limit. Germany's Autobahn advisory speed is 130 km/h, though many sections have no legal maximum. At Mach 0.105, a car at this speed travels at about 10.5% the speed of sound.

## Assumptions & Limitations

- **Exact conversion factors.** All conversions use the internationally defined relationships between units. No rounding is applied to the conversion factors themselves — only the displayed result is rounded for readability.
- **Mach number is temperature-dependent.** This converter uses Mach 1 = 343 m/s, which is the speed of sound in dry air at 20°C and sea-level pressure (101.325 kPa). At higher altitudes or different temperatures, the actual speed of sound changes, and Mach conversions would differ accordingly.
- **Speed of light is exact.** The value 299,792,458 m/s is a defined constant in the SI system, not a measurement. Conversions to and from the speed of light are exact.
- **Positive values only.** Speed magnitude is always non-negative. Direction is not modeled.
