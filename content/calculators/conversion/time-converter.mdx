## The Formula

$$\text{Converted Value} = \text{Value} \times \frac{\text{From Factor}}{\text{To Factor}}$$

Where each unit's factor represents seconds:

- **Seconds** = 1
- **Minutes** = 60
- **Hours** = 3,600
- **Days** = 86,400
- **Weeks** = 604,800
- **Months** = 2,629,746 (30.44 days average)

The month uses 30.44 days (365.25 / 12) and the year uses 365.25 days to account for leap years, following the Julian year convention used in astronomy and the International System of Units (SI).

### Converting 3 Days to All Units

A project manager needs to express a 3-day deadline in multiple time units for international team members who track time differently.

**Step 1 — Convert to base (seconds):**

**Step 2 — Convert to each unit:**

- Minutes: 259,200 / 60 = **4,320 minutes**

## Key Factors in Time Conversion

**Month and year lengths vary.** Calendar months range from 28 to 31 days, and years are 365 or 366 days. This converter uses statistical averages (30.44 days/month, 365.25 days/year) that are standard in scientific and financial contexts. For calendar-specific calculations — such as "how many days until March 15?" — use a date calculator instead.

**Precision matters in technical fields.** In GPS, a microsecond (0.000001 seconds) corresponds to about 300 meters of positioning error. In high-frequency trading, nanosecond differences determine order execution. This converter provides six decimal places, sufficient for most engineering and scientific work.

**Leap seconds and time zones are not included.** This converter uses fixed conversion factors and does not account for UTC leap seconds (27 have been added since 1972) or daylight saving time shifts. For timezone-aware or calendar-aware conversions, use a dedicated date/time calculator.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How many seconds are in a year?

A Julian year contains 31,557,600 seconds (365.25 days x 86,400 seconds/day). A common calendar year has 31,536,000 seconds (365 days), while a leap year has 31,622,400 seconds (366 days). The 365.25-day average is the standard used in astronomy and the SI system.

### Why does this converter use 30.44 days for a month?

The 30.44-day month (more precisely, 30.436875 days) comes from dividing the Julian year (365.25 days) by 12 months. This average accounts for the mix of 28, 29, 30, and 31-day months across the calendar year and is the standard conversion factor used in scientific and financial calculations.

### How do I convert weeks to months accurately?

Using the standard factors: 1 week = 604,800 seconds and 1 month = 2,629,746 seconds, so 1 week = 0.2300 months. For 4 weeks: 4 x 0.2300 = 0.9201 months — slightly less than 1 calendar month. For calendar-precise results, count actual days between specific dates instead.
