## Your Result Explained

The greatest common factor (GCF) and least common multiple (LCM) are the two fundamental operations in number theory for relating pairs of integers. They appear constantly in everyday math — simplifying fractions, finding common denominators, scheduling recurring events, and dividing quantities evenly.

**GCF (Greatest Common Factor)** — also called GCD (greatest common divisor) or HCF (highest common factor) — is the largest number that divides evenly into all given numbers. GCF(12, 18) = 6 because 6 is the largest number that divides both 12 and 18 without a remainder. This is what you use to simplify fractions: 12/18 reduces to 2/3 by dividing both numerator and denominator by their GCF of 6.

**LCM (Least Common Multiple)** is the smallest positive number that is a multiple of all given numbers. LCM(4, 6) = 12 because 12 is the first number that appears in both the multiples of 4 (4, 8, 12, 16...) and the multiples of 6 (6, 12, 18...). This is what you use to find common denominators when adding fractions: 1/4 + 1/6 requires a denominator of LCM(4, 6) = 12.

**Prime Factorization** breaks each number into its prime building blocks. This calculator shows the factorization for each input number, which visually demonstrates why the GCF and LCM have their values — the GCF uses the minimum exponent for each shared prime, while the LCM uses the maximum exponent.

## The Formula

### Euclidean Algorithm for GCF

$$\gcd(a, b) = \gcd(b, \; a \bmod b) \quad \text{until } b = 0, \text{ then } \gcd = a$$

Example: gcd(48, 18) = gcd(18, 12) = gcd(12, 6) = gcd(6, 0) = **6**

### LCM via GCF Relationship

$$\text{LCM}(a, b) = \frac{|a \times b|}{\gcd(a, b)}$$

### Product Identity

$$a \times b = \gcd(a, b) \times \text{LCM}(a, b)$$

This identity provides a useful verification: 12 x 18 = 216 = 6 x 36 = 216.

### Three-Number Extension

$$\gcd(a, b, c) = \gcd(\gcd(a, b), \; c)$$

$$\text{LCM}(a, b, c) = \text{LCM}(\text{LCM}(a, b), \; c)$$

Where:

- **gcd(a, b)** = greatest common divisor (same as GCF)
- **a mod b** = the remainder when a is divided by b
- **|a x b|** = absolute value of the product (handles negative inputs)

**Source:** Euclidean algorithm from Euclid's *Elements*, Book VII, Propositions 1-2 (circa 300 BC). The GCF-LCM product identity is a standard result in elementary number theory, documented in Hardy & Wright's *An Introduction to the Theory of Numbers* (Oxford University Press).

## Worked Example

### Finding GCF and LCM of 48 and 36

You need to simplify the fraction 48/36 and find the LCD for adding fractions with denominators 48 and 36.

**Step-by-step:**

1. **Prime factorization:**
   - 48 = 2 x 2 x 2 x 2 x 3 = **2^4 x 3**
   - 36 = 2 x 2 x 3 x 3 = **2^2 x 3^2**

2. **Find GCF** (take minimum exponents of shared primes):
   - For 2: min(4, 2) = 2 -> 2^2 = 4
   - For 3: min(1, 2) = 1 -> 3^1 = 3
   - GCF = 4 x 3 = **12**

3. **Find LCM** (take maximum exponents of all primes):
   - For 2: max(4, 2) = 4 -> 2^4 = 16
   - For 3: max(1, 2) = 2 -> 3^2 = 9
   - LCM = 16 x 9 = **144**

4. **Verify:** 48 x 36 = 1,728 = 12 x 144 = 1,728

**Practical interpretation:** The fraction 48/36 simplifies to 4/3 (dividing both by GCF 12). To add fractions with denominators 48 and 36, use LCD = LCM = 144. Use our [Fraction Calculator](/calculators/math/fraction-calculator) to perform the actual addition once you have the common denominator.

## How This Is Calculated

This calculator finds the greatest common factor using the Euclidean algorithm — repeatedly dividing the larger number by the smaller and taking the remainder until it reaches zero. The least common multiple is derived from the relationship LCM(a, b) = |a × b| / GCF(a, b). For more than two numbers, the calculator applies these operations iteratively across the set.
