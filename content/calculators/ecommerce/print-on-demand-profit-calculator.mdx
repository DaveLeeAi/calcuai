## What Your Results Mean

**Net Profit per Order** is what you keep after the POD provider's charge, platform fee, shipping, and any ad spend.

**Profit Margin** is net profit as a percentage of selling price. POD margins typically run 20–40% — lower than private label but with zero inventory risk.

**Monthly Profit** projects your income at your order volume. Because POD has no inventory overhead, all profit is effectively cash flow.

## How to Use This Calculator

1. Enter your **Selling Price** — what your customer pays
2. Enter **POD Cost per Item** — what your provider charges (base product + printing). Check Printful/Printify's pricing calculator for exact figures.
3. Enter **Shipping Cost** — what you pay for delivery to the customer (0 if free shipping is built into POD cost)
4. Select your **Selling Platform** — pre-fills fees for Etsy, Shopify, and others
5. For **Amazon Merch** or **Redbubble**: enter the royalty % you keep in the custom rate field
6. Enter **Ad Spend per Order** and **Monthly Orders** for projections

## Print-on-Demand Profit Formula

<div className="formula-section">

**Standard platforms (Etsy, Shopify):**

$$
\text{Platform Fee} = \text{Selling Price} \times \text{Fee Rate} + \text{Fixed Fee}
$$

$$
\text{Net Profit} = \text{Selling Price} - \text{POD Cost} - \text{Shipping} - \text{Platform Fee} - \text{Ad Spend}
$$

**Royalty platforms (Amazon Merch, Redbubble):**

$$
\text{Your Revenue} = \text{Selling Price} \times \text{Royalty\%}
$$

$$
\text{Net Profit} = \text{Your Revenue} - \text{POD Cost} - \text{Shipping} - \text{Ad Spend}
$$

**Platform Fee Rates:**

| Platform | Effective Fee Rate |
|----------|--------------------|
| Etsy | ~9.5% + $0.45 (6.5% txn + 3% processing + $0.20 listing + $0.25 fixed) |
| Shopify Payments (Basic) | 2.9% + $0.30 |
| Amazon Merch | You keep ~13–37% of list price (enter as royalty%) |
| Redbubble | You set markup; keep 20% of that markup |

Source: Printful — Pricing and Profit Calculator (2025); Etsy — Fees & Payments Policy (2025).

## Worked Example 1 — Printful T-Shirt on Etsy

A designer sells a Bella+Canvas 3001 unisex tee printed by Printful on Etsy for **$34.99**. Printful charges $13.95 for the shirt + print. Shipping $4.50. No paid ads.

| Cost Item | Amount |
|-----------|--------|
| Printful Cost (shirt + print) | $13.95 |
| Shipping to Customer | $4.50 |
| Etsy Transaction Fee (6.5%) | $2.27 |
| Etsy Payment Processing (3%+) | $1.30 |
| Etsy Listing Renewal | $0.20 |
| **Net Profit** | **$12.77** |
| **Profit Margin** | **36.5%** |

At 60 orders/month: **$766/month** with zero inventory held.

## Key Factors That Affect POD Profit

**POD base costs vary significantly by provider.** Printful is typically 15–20% more expensive than Printify for the same product because Printful handles fulfillment in-house (faster, more consistent quality). Printify uses a network of print providers (prices vary by provider). For high-volume shops, Printify's lower cost can add $2–4 per order back to your margin.

**Shipping is your second-largest cost after the base product.** Printful charges $3.99–$6.99 for standard US domestic shipping on most apparel. Offering free shipping increases conversion but compresses margin. Test both approaches before committing.

**Amazon Merch royalties are non-intuitive.** You don't pay Amazon a fee — Amazon produces, ships, and handles customer service. Your "royalty" is what Amazon pays you after deducting production cost and their margin. On a $19.99 t-shirt, Amazon might pay a royalty of $2–$4. The calculation is: Royalty = List Price − $0.30 − Amazon's production cost − Amazon's margin. Enter your expected royalty % in the calculator's custom rate field.

**Seasonal demand creates cash flow peaks.** POD businesses often see 3–5× order volume in November–December. Because there's no inventory risk, this is pure upside — but verify your POD provider's fulfillment SLAs before holiday season.

**Design licensing.** Using licensed characters, brand logos, or trademarked phrases in POD products violates IP law and platform terms. Merch by Amazon suspends accounts permanently for IP violations. Stick to original designs.

## Assumptions & Limitations

- POD cost is the all-in charge from your provider including blank product + printing. Check your specific product in the provider's pricing calculator as rates vary by product, provider, and print area.
- Etsy fee shown (~9.5% + $0.45) combines transaction fee (6.5%), payment processing (3%), and listing renewal ($0.20), plus the $0.25 per-order processing fixed fee.
- Amazon Merch royalty model is highly variable — use your actual royalty rate or estimate conservatively (20–30% of list price for most tiers).
- Shipping costs not charged to buyer are treated as a seller cost in this model.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**What is a good profit margin for print on demand?**
A healthy POD margin is 25–40% of the selling price. Margins below 20% leave little room for ad spend or returns. Margins above 40% are achievable for digital-heavy designs (all-over-print, premium positioning) but require higher selling prices that may limit volume. Most successful POD shops target $8–$15 net profit per unit.

**Printful vs Printify — which is more profitable?**
Printify is generally 15–25% cheaper on base costs, which directly improves profit margin. Printful commands a premium for more consistent quality, faster shipping, and simpler management. For shops prioritizing margin at volume, Printify wins. For shops where quality consistency and customer experience justify the premium, Printful is worth it.

**Is print on demand worth it on Etsy vs Shopify?**
Etsy offers built-in traffic but takes ~9.5% + $0.45 in fees per order. Shopify has lower fees (~3.2% effective) but requires paid traffic or strong SEO. For beginners, Etsy's organic discovery is worth the fee premium — it removes the need for an ad budget while you build a catalog. Scaling sellers often move high-performing designs to Shopify to capture the fee savings.

**How does Merch by Amazon work for profit?**
Amazon Merch is an invitation-only POD program. You upload designs; Amazon handles production, pricing, shipping, and customer service. You earn a royalty (typically $1.50–$5 per shirt depending on tier and price). You have no control over production costs — your only lever is the list price. It's low-effort but low-margin compared to Etsy or Shopify POD.
