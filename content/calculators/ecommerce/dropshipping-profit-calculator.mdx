## What Your Results Mean

**Net Profit per Order** is what you earn after supplier cost, shipping, platform fee, and ad spend are all deducted from the selling price.

**Profit Margin** is net profit as a percentage of selling price — sustainable dropshipping businesses typically target 20–40%.

**ROI** measures return on your cash outlay (supplier + shipping + ads). A 100% ROI means you doubled the money you spent.

**Break-Even Selling Price** is the minimum price you must charge to cover all costs — useful for competitive pricing research.

## How to Use This Calculator

1. Enter your **Selling Price** — what your customer pays
2. Enter your **Supplier Cost** — what you pay AliExpress, CJ Dropshipping, or your supplier per order
3. Enter **Shipping Cost** — supplier's shipping charge (0 if included in supplier cost)
4. Select your **Selling Platform** — pre-fills the fee rate for common platforms
5. Enter **Ad Spend per Order** — your monthly ad budget ÷ monthly orders
6. Enter **Monthly Orders** for your income projection

## Dropshipping Profit Formula

<div className="formula-section">

$$
\text{Platform Fee} = \text{Selling Price} \times \text{Platform Rate} + \text{Fixed Fee}
$$

$$
\text{Net Profit} = \text{Selling Price} - \text{Supplier Cost} - \text{Shipping} - \text{Platform Fee} - \text{Ad Spend}
$$

$$
\text{Profit Margin} = \frac{\text{Net Profit}}{\text{Selling Price}} \times 100
$$

$$
\text{ROI} = \frac{\text{Net Profit}}{\text{Supplier Cost} + \text{Shipping} + \text{Ad Spend}} \times 100
$$

$$
\text{Break-Even Price} = \frac{\text{Supplier} + \text{Shipping} + \text{Ad Spend} + \text{Fixed Fee}}{1 - \text{Platform Rate}}
$$

A dropshipper sells a kitchen gadget for **$44.99** with a $13 AliExpress cost, $3.50 supplier shipping, and $7 Facebook ad spend per order. Using Shopify Payments Basic.

| Cost Item | Amount |
|-----------|--------|
| Supplier Cost | $13.00 |
| Supplier Shipping | $3.50 |

| **Total Costs** | **$25.11** |
| **Net Profit** | **$19.88** |
| **Profit Margin** | **44.2%** |

With 150 orders/month, that's **$2,982/month** in net profit.

## Key Factors That Affect Dropshipping Profit

**Ad cost per order is the biggest variable.** A business spending $10/order on ads at 30% ROAS is fundamentally different from one spending $3/order on organic TikTok traffic. Track your actual cost-per-purchase in your ad platform and plug it in — don't estimate.

**Supplier shipping delays kill refund rates.** ePacket and AliExpress Standard Shipping can take 15–45 days. A 5% dispute/refund rate at $44.99 costs you $2.25 per order sold — equivalent to adding another $2.25 to your ad spend. Budget for it.

**Platform transaction fees on Shopify.** If you use a third-party payment processor instead of Shopify Payments, Shopify charges an additional 1–2% transaction fee on top of the processor's fee. The calculator models this in the Shopify + Stripe option (adds 1% Shopify transaction fee to Stripe's 2.9%).

**Product-market fit determines scalability.** A 40% margin at 50 orders/month is not the same as 40% margin at 2,000 orders/month if the supplier runs out of stock, raises prices, or quality drops at volume.

**Chargeback rate.** High-ticket dropshipping products see chargeback rates of 0.5–2%. Chargebacks cost the disputed sale amount plus a $15–$25 processing fee. Include this in margin planning for any item over $100.

## Assumptions & Limitations

- Supplier cost is the per-unit price paid to your dropship supplier — this calculator assumes you pay per order, not with minimum quantities.
- Shipping cost is what your supplier charges you (may be free on AliExpress for certain shipping methods).
- Platform fees shown are for the base tier; higher plans have different rates.
- Ad spend per order should be your actual blended CPA (cost per acquisition) not your CPM or CPC.
- Returns and chargebacks are not modeled — factor in 2–5% of revenue for high-risk categories.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**What is a good profit margin for dropshipping?**
A sustainable dropshipping profit margin is 20–40% of selling price. Margins below 15% are difficult to sustain once you account for refunds, chargebacks, and ad volatility. Margins above 40% are achievable with winning products and low-cost traffic sources (organic social, SEO), but are hard to maintain at scale.

**How do I calculate ad spend per order for the calculator?**
Divide your total monthly ad spend by your monthly orders. If you spent $1,500 on Facebook ads and got 200 orders, your ad cost per order is $7.50. Use your actual 30-day average rather than estimating — ad costs vary significantly by product and audience.

**Is dropshipping on Amazon profitable?**
Dropshipping on Amazon is legal but challenging due to Amazon's 15% referral fee (vs. ~3% on Shopify) and strict policies: you must be the seller of record, not ship from a marketplace (like AliExpress), and not have the supplier's name on packing materials. Margins are tighter and the risk of account suspension is higher than Shopify dropshipping.

**What platforms have the lowest fees for dropshipping?**
Shopify Payments has the lowest fees at 2.9% + $0.30 with no additional transaction fee (2.9–3.5% effective depending on AOV). WooCommerce with Stripe is comparable. eBay (13.25% FVF + 2.9% processing) and Amazon (15%) take substantially more, making them harder platforms for thin-margin dropship products.
