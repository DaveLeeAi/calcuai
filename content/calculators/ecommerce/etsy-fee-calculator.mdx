## What Your Results Mean

**Net Profit per Order** is what you take home after Etsy's 6.5% transaction fee, 3% + $0.25 payment processing, $0.20 listing renewal, optional offsite ads fee, your actual shipping cost, and your product cost.

**Profit Margin** is net profit as a percentage of your item price — sustainable Etsy shops typically run 30–50% before any paid marketing.

**Monthly Profit** projects your take at your sales volume, helping you decide whether to scale a listing or retire it.

## How to Use This Calculator

1. Enter your **Item Price** — what buyers pay for the item (excluding shipping)
2. Enter your **Product Cost** — materials, supplies, or wholesale cost
3. Enter **Shipping Charged to Buyer** — Etsy charges its transaction fee on item + shipping
4. Enter **Your Actual Shipping Cost** — what you pay for postage
5. Set **Sales from Offsite Ads %** — the fraction of sales driven by Etsy's off-platform ads
6. Select **Annual Revenue** — determines whether your offsite ads rate is 12% or 15%
7. Enter **Monthly Orders** for your income projection

## Etsy Fee Formula

<div className="formula-section">

Etsy's transaction fee applies to **both the item price and shipping charged to the buyer** — a detail many sellers miss when pricing.

$$
\text{Fee Base} = \text{Item Price} + \text{Shipping Charged to Buyer}
$$

$$
\text{Transaction Fee} = \text{Fee Base} \times 6.5\%
$$

$$
\text{Payment Processing} = \text{Fee Base} \times 3\% + \$0.25
$$

$$
\text{Listing Fee} = \$0.20 \text{ (auto-renewal per sale)}
$$

$$
\text{Offsite Ads Fee} = \text{Item Price} \times \text{Offsite \%} \times \text{Rate (12\% or 15\%)}
$$

$$
\text{Net Profit} = \text{Item Price} - \text{Transaction} - \text{Processing} - \text{Listing} - \text{Offsite Ads} - \text{Actual Shipping} - \text{COGS}
$$

**Offsite Ads Rate:**
- Shops earning **under $10,000/year**: optional enrollment, 15% fee on qualifying sales
- Shops earning **$10,000+/year**: mandatory enrollment, 12% fee on qualifying sales

Source: Etsy — Fees & Payments Policy (2025); Etsy — Offsite Ads policy (2025).

## Worked Example 1 — Handmade Candle on Etsy (No Offsite Ads)

A candle maker sells a 8oz soy candle for **$22.00** with $5 buyer shipping. Actual shipping cost is $4.25. Materials cost $5.50.

| Fee | Calculation | Amount |
|-----|-------------|--------|
| Fee Base | $22.00 + $5.00 | $27.00 |
| Transaction Fee (6.5%) | $27.00 × 6.5% | $1.76 |
| Payment Processing (3%+) | $27.00 × 3% + $0.25 | $1.06 |
| Listing Fee | auto-renewal | $0.20 |
| Actual Shipping | (USPS First Class) | $4.25 |
| Materials Cost | — | $5.50 |
| **Net Profit** | $22.00 − $1.76 − $1.06 − $0.20 − $4.25 − $5.50 | **$9.23** |
| **Profit Margin** | $9.23 / $22.00 | **42%** |

## Worked Example 2 — Digital Print with Offsite Ads

A shop selling digital art prints ($19.99 instant download, no shipping) earns $25,000/year on Etsy. 30% of sales come through Offsite Ads (mandatory at 12%).

| Fee | Calculation | Amount |
|-----|-------------|--------|
| Transaction Fee (6.5%) | $19.99 × 6.5% | $1.30 |
| Payment Processing (3%) | $19.99 × 3% + $0.25 | $0.85 |

Digital products have exceptional margins on Etsy — no shipping, no materials — but the mandatory Offsite Ads fee applies to successful shops.

## Key Factors That Affect Etsy Profit

**The listing renewal fee adds up at volume.** Each sale auto-renews the listing for $0.20. At 100 orders per month, that's $20/month — minor individually but real at scale.

**Transaction fee on shipping creates pricing traps.** If you charge $8 for shipping on a $20 item, Etsy charges 6.5% of $28 = $1.82 rather than $1.30 on the item alone. Some sellers build shipping into the item price and list as "free shipping" to avoid this compounding, though they absorb the actual shipping cost.

**Offsite Ads threshold is $10K trailing 12 months.** Once you cross it, Etsy automatically enrolls you in Offsite Ads at 12% — you cannot opt out. Plan your pricing to account for this when you're approaching that threshold.

**Star Seller badge affects visibility.** While not a direct fee, maintaining Star Seller status (95%+ 5-star reviews, under 0.3% case rate, message response within 24h) influences search ranking, which affects effective conversion rate and ad efficiency.

**Currency conversion.** If you sell internationally, Etsy charges an additional 2.5% currency conversion fee on international transactions — this calculator assumes USD-to-USD sales.

## Assumptions & Limitations

- Rates are for **Etsy Payments in the US** (2025). International sellers may pay different processing rates.
- Listing fee modeled as $0.20 per sale (auto-renewal when item sells). Initial listing fee also $0.20 but typically amortized across multiple sales for multi-quantity listings.
- Offsite Ads fee applies only to sales that originate from an Etsy Offsite Ad — organic Etsy search sales are not charged.
- Pattern (Etsy's website builder) costs $15/month and is not included in per-order calculations.
- UK, EU, and Canadian sellers face different processing rates and regulatory fees.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**How much does Etsy charge per sale?**
Etsy charges a minimum of $0.20 (listing renewal) + 6.5% transaction fee + 3% + $0.25 payment processing per sale, applied to the item price plus shipping charged to the buyer. On a $25 item with $5 shipping, total Etsy fees are approximately $3.15 before your shipping and product costs.

**What is the Etsy transaction fee charged on?**
Etsy's 6.5% transaction fee is charged on the item price plus any shipping amount collected from the buyer. If you charge $5 for shipping on a $25 item, the fee base is $30 and the transaction fee is $1.95 — not $1.63 on the item alone. This is why many high-volume Etsy sellers include shipping in the item price.

**Is Etsy Offsite Ads mandatory?**
Offsite Ads are optional for shops with less than $10,000 in annual sales. Once a shop crosses the $10,000 threshold in the last 12 months, participation becomes mandatory at a 12% fee rate. Shops under $10K can opt in voluntarily at a 15% rate. The fee only applies to sales that came through Etsy's off-platform advertising.

**Can you avoid Etsy fees?**
No — Etsy's transaction and payment processing fees apply to every sale. You can minimize fees by pricing strategically, not charging separate shipping (so buyers don't contribute to the fee base), and maintaining high conversion rates to reduce wasted listing fees. Etsy Plus ($10/month) provides listing credits and ad credits that can partially offset fees at certain volumes.
