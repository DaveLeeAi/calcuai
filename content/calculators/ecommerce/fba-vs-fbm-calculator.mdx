## What Your Results Mean

**FBA Net Profit** is profit per unit using Amazon's fulfillment network: referral fee + FBA fee + inbound shipping + COGS deducted from selling price.

**FBM Net Profit** is profit per unit when you ship direct to customers: referral fee + your shipping cost + packaging/labor + COGS deducted.

**Recommendation** identifies which method produces higher per-unit profit based on your inputs — though Buy Box eligibility, Prime badge access, and storage fees can override the raw math.

## How to Use This Calculator

1. Enter your **Selling Price** and **Product Cost** (landed COGS)
2. Select your **Amazon Category** — determines referral fee % (applies equally to both methods)
3. Select your **FBA Size Tier** — determines the FBA fulfillment fee
4. Enter **FBA Inbound Shipping** — cost to ship inventory from your supplier to Amazon's warehouse (per unit)
5. Enter **FBM Shipping to Customer** — your cost per shipment (USPS, UPS, etc.)
6. Enter **FBM Packaging & Labor** — box, tape, dunnage, packing time per order

## FBA vs FBM Formula

<div className="formula-section">

Both methods pay the same Amazon referral fee — the difference is entirely in fulfillment cost structure.

$$
\text{Referral Fee} = \text{Selling Price} \times \text{Category Rate}
$$

$$
\text{FBA Net Profit} = \text{Price} - \text{Referral Fee} - \text{FBA Fee} - \text{Inbound Shipping} - \text{COGS}
$$

$$
\text{FBM Net Profit} = \text{Price} - \text{Referral Fee} - \text{FBM Shipping} - \text{Packaging} - \text{COGS}
$$

## Worked Example 1 — Small Item Where FBA Wins

A kitchen sponge holder ($24.99, large standard 6 oz, Home & Garden 15% category). Inbound shipping: $1.00/unit. FBM alternative: $5.50 shipping + $1.25 packaging.

| Metric | FBA | FBM |
|--------|-----|-----|
| Referral Fee (15%) | $3.75 | $3.75 |
| Fulfillment Fee | $4.08 | $6.75 |
| Inbound / Packaging | $1.00 | $1.25 |
| COGS | $5.00 | $5.00 |
| **Net Profit** | **$11.16** | **$8.25** |
| **Margin** | 44.7% | 33.0% |

FBA wins by **$2.91/unit** because the FBA fee ($4.08) is cheaper than the real-world cost to ship and package ($6.75 + $1.25).

## Worked Example 2 — Bulky Item Where FBM Wins

A heavy cast iron skillet ($59.99, Small Oversize, Kitchen 15% category). FBA fee: $9.73. Inbound shipping: $4.00/unit (heavy). FBM: $8.50 Priority Mail + $2.00 packaging.

| Metric | FBA | FBM |
|--------|-----|-----|
| Referral Fee (15%) | $9.00 | $9.00 |
| Fulfillment Fee | $9.73 | $10.50 |
| Inbound / Packaging | $4.00 | $2.00 |

| **Net Profit** | **$19.27** | **$20.50** |
| **Margin** | 32.1% | 34.2% |

FBM wins by **$1.23/unit** here — but FBA still offers Prime eligibility and removes the fulfillment burden.

## Key Factors Beyond the Raw Math

**The Prime badge changes everything.** FBA products are Prime-eligible by default. FBM sellers can qualify for Seller Fulfilled Prime (SFP), but it requires same-day/next-day shipping with 99%+ on-time delivery — operationally demanding. Without Prime, FBM items rank lower in search and convert at a lower rate than FBA equivalents, often erasing any per-unit profit advantage.

**Storage fees are a hidden FBA cost.** This calculator shows per-unit fulfillment — but FBA also charges monthly storage: $0.78/cubic foot (Jan–Sep) and $2.40/cubic foot (Oct–Dec). Slow-moving FBA inventory accumulates storage fees that can turn a profitable-per-unit product into a net loss. Use the [Amazon FBA Storage Fee Calculator](/ecommerce/amazon-fba-storage-fee-calculator) alongside this one.

**Returns are processed differently.** FBA handles returns automatically — customers ship back to Amazon and get automatic refunds. For FBM, you manage returns yourself. FBA returns can sometimes result in unsellable inventory that Amazon disposes of or charges you to reship.

**FBM gives you inventory flexibility.** FBM inventory stored at your own warehouse or 3PL can be listed on multiple channels simultaneously (your website, eBay, Etsy) without splitting stock. FBA inventory is locked to Amazon.

**Inbound shipping costs are rising.** Amazon's Inbound Placement Fee (introduced in 2024) means shipments sent to a single FBA warehouse are redistributed by Amazon at additional cost. Shipping to multiple warehouses or using Amazon Warehousing & Distribution (AWD) can reduce per-unit inbound costs.

## Assumptions & Limitations

- Referral fee rates are from Amazon's 2026 category schedule. A small number of categories have tiered rates not shown here.
- FBA fees are from Amazon's January 15, 2026 effective schedule. Fees are updated periodically.
- FBM shipping costs should reflect your actual carrier rates for the specific product weight/dimensions — use your shipping carrier's rate calculator for accuracy.
- Monthly storage fees, aged inventory surcharges, returns, and long-term storage fees are not included in per-unit calculation.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Should I use FBA or FBM as a new Amazon seller?**
Most new sellers start with FBA because it handles logistics, enables Prime eligibility, and reduces operational complexity. FBM makes more sense for heavy or oversized products where FBA fulfillment fees exceed self-shipping costs, for seasonal items where FBA storage fees would be prohibitive, or for sellers with an existing fulfillment operation who want multi-channel flexibility.

**Can I use both FBA and FBM at the same time?**
Yes — you can list the same ASIN with both an FBA offer and an FBM offer simultaneously (called a "multi-channel listing"). This is useful as a backup if your FBA inventory runs out. Amazon's algorithm will typically favor the FBA offer for the Buy Box, but having both prevents losing sales to stockouts.

**What is the FBA inbound shipping fee?**
Amazon charges an Inbound Placement Fee for FBA shipments sent to a single fulfillment center (Amazon redistributes inventory internally). Fees range from $0.27–$1.58 per unit depending on size tier and destination. Shipping to multiple fulfillment centers yourself or using Amazon's partnered carrier rates can minimize this cost.

**Does FBM affect the Buy Box?**
FBM sellers can win the Buy Box if they maintain competitive pricing, excellent seller metrics (ODR under 1%, pre-fulfillment cancel rate under 2.5%, late shipment rate under 4%), and offer fast shipping. However, FBA sellers have an inherent advantage because Prime eligibility and Amazon's fulfillment reputation factor into Buy Box calculations.
