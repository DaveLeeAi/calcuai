## Reading Your TikTok Shop Numbers

**Net Profit per Order** is what you keep on each TikTok Shop sale after all platform fees, affiliate commission, shipping, and COGS are deducted. This is the only number that matters for unit economics — not gross revenue, not gross margin before fees.

**Profit Margin** expresses net profit as a percentage of the selling price. On TikTok Shop, a healthy margin is typically 25–40%. Lower margins compress fast when affiliates renegotiate rates or TikTok adjusts fee policy. Higher margins (40%+) create room for higher affiliate commission to drive more creator promotion.

**Monthly Profit** is net profit per order multiplied by your monthly order volume. This is the business-level number — the figure that matters for cash flow planning, inventory financing, and understanding whether the channel is worth the operational overhead of managing affiliate relationships.

**Fee Breakdown Table** shows every deduction as a dollar amount and percentage of sale price, so you can immediately identify the highest-cost line item. For most TikTok Shop sellers, affiliate commission is the largest variable — and also the most controllable lever.

## How to Use This Calculator

1. **Enter Selling Price** — your TikTok Shop list price for the product. If you run discount offers, use the discounted price (that is what fees are calculated on).
2. **Enter Product Cost (COGS)** — your fully landed cost per unit. If you import from overseas, use your landed cost (including duties and freight), not your factory price. See our [Landed Cost Calculator](/ecommerce/landed-cost-calculator) to derive this number accurately.
3. **Select Product Category** — determines your platform commission rate. Beauty, Fashion, Health, Home, Food, Sports, Toys, and Pets are all 8%. Electronics is 5%. "Other" defaults to 8%.
4. **Enter Affiliate Commission %** — the percentage you pay to TikTok creators who promote your product. Standard creator commissions on TikTok Shop run 5–25% depending on category and product. Mass-market consumer goods with high repurchase rates typically pay 10–15%. Hero-launch products may pay 20%+ to incentivize top creators.
5. **Enter Shipping Cost** — your cost to ship the order to the customer. TikTok Shop sellers generally handle their own fulfillment (unlike Amazon FBA). If you use 3PL, enter the per-order pick-pack-ship cost.
6. **Enter Monthly Orders** — your current or projected order volume. This converts per-unit economics into a monthly profit figure.

## The Formula

<div className="formula-section">

**Platform Commission:**

$$
\text{Platform Commission} = \text{Selling Price} \times \text{Category Rate\%}
$$

**Transaction Fee:**

$$
\text{Transaction Fee} = \text{Selling Price} \times 3\%
$$

**Net Profit:**

$$
\text{Net Profit} = \text{Price} - \text{Commission} - \text{Affiliate Fee} - \text{Transaction Fee} - \text{Small Order Fee} - \text{Shipping} - \text{COGS}
$$

**Profit Margin:**

$$
\text{Profit Margin\%} = \frac{\text{Net Profit}}{\text{Selling Price}} \times 100
$$

Where:
- **Category Rate%** = TikTok Shop platform commission by product category (5% electronics, 8% all others)
- **Small Order Fee** = \$2.00 flat fee applied only when selling price is below \$10.00
- **Affiliate Fee** = Selling Price × your negotiated affiliate commission percentage
- **COGS** = fully landed product cost per unit

*Source: TikTok Shop — US Seller Fee Policy (2025). TikTok Shop — Commission Rate Schedule by Category (2025).*

## Worked Examples

### Example 1: Beauty Serum (Typical TikTok Shop Launch)

A DTC brand launches a vitamin C serum on TikTok Shop at \$28.99. Landed COGS: \$7.50. Affiliate commission: 12%.

| Cost Component | Amount | % of Sale |
|---|---|---|
| Selling Price | \$28.99 | 100% |
| Platform Commission (8%) | −\$2.32 | 8.0% |
| Affiliate Commission (12%) | −\$3.48 | 12.0% |
| Transaction Fee (3%) | −\$0.87 | 3.0% |
| Shipping Cost | −\$3.50 | 12.1% |
| Product Cost (COGS) | −\$7.50 | 25.9% |
| **Net Profit** | **\$11.32** | **39.1%** |

*Interpretation:* At 39% margin, this product has strong unit economics. The brand can afford to push affiliate rates to 15–17% to recruit top creators and still clear 30% net margin. At 500 orders/month, monthly profit is \$5,660.

### Example 2: Low-Price Item (Small Order Fee Impact)

A seller lists a pack of 3 cable organizers at \$7.99 with \$2.00 COGS and 8% affiliate commission.

| Cost Component | Amount |
|---|---|
| Selling Price | \$7.99 |
| Platform Commission (8%) | −\$0.64 |
| Affiliate Commission (8%) | −\$0.64 |
| Transaction Fee (3%) | −\$0.24 |
| Small Order Fee | −\$2.00 |
| Shipping | −\$2.50 |
| Product Cost | −\$2.00 |
| **Net Profit** | **\$−2.03** |

*Interpretation:* The \$2.00 small order fee (applied to all orders below \$10.00) makes sub-\$10 products nearly impossible to profit on. This seller has two options: raise the price above \$10.00 to eliminate the small order fee, or bundle into a larger pack (e.g., 10-pack at \$19.99) where the fee disappears and margins improve dramatically. Selling below \$10 on TikTok Shop requires a specific strategy — it is not viable as a default.

## What Eats Into TikTok Shop Margins

1. **Affiliate Commission Is the Largest Lever** — Unlike Amazon where fees are mostly fixed, TikTok Shop gives you complete control over affiliate commission. The difference between 5% and 20% affiliate rate on a \$30 product is \$4.50 per order. A 5,000-order month swing at \$4.50 = \$22,500/month difference. This is why negotiating creator rates strategically — starting lower for top creators who drive volume, higher for niche creators who drive discovery — is the most important margin management skill on TikTok Shop.

2. **The \$10 Small Order Fee Threshold** — The flat \$2.00 fee on orders below \$10.00 is a hard product viability threshold. At \$7.99, \$2.00 represents 25% of selling price before any other fee. Products priced at \$9.99 or below need to be bundled, repriced, or left off TikTok Shop entirely unless you have a specific use case (loss-leader traffic builder) for that price point.

3. **Electronics Category Discount** — Electronics products pay only 5% platform commission vs. 8% for all other categories. On a \$50 electronic product, that 3% difference saves \$1.50/unit. For high-volume electronics sellers, this is meaningful — especially because electronics often pair with lower affiliate commission requirements (consumers research independently, making creator-driven discovery less dominant).

4. **3% Transaction Fee Is Non-Negotiable** — The 3% payment processing fee applies to all orders regardless of plan or category. Combined with the 8% commission, TikTok Shop's minimum fee floor for non-electronics is 11% of selling price before affiliate and shipping. Sellers quoting "8% commission" without accounting for transaction fees are systematically undercounting platform cost by \$0.75+ per \$25 order.

5. **Seller-Managed Fulfillment vs. Platform Fulfillment** — TikTok Shop offers platform-managed fulfillment (TikTok fulfillment service) in some markets. If you use this, the shipping cost in the calculator would represent your fulfillment fee rather than direct shipping. Model both scenarios before committing to either fulfillment approach, as fulfillment cost structures differ significantly.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What fees does TikTok Shop charge sellers?

TikTok Shop charges three platform fees: (1) Platform commission of 5–8% depending on product category, (2) Transaction fee of 3% on all orders, and (3) Small order fee of \$2.00 flat on orders below \$10.00. These are separate from any affiliate commission you pay to creators, which is set by you and represents an additional variable cost typically ranging from 5–25% of selling price.

### How does TikTok Shop commission compare to Amazon?

Amazon referral fees run 8–17% by category. TikTok Shop's platform commission alone is 5–8%, but the 3% transaction fee brings the minimum to 8–11%. For most product categories, total platform fees are comparable — Amazon is often slightly cheaper in platform fees alone. The difference is TikTok's additional affiliate commission (5–25%), which Amazon does not have. However, TikTok affiliates provide free organic marketing that would otherwise require paid ads on Amazon, making the comparison more nuanced than raw fee percentages.

### Is TikTok Shop profitable for sub-\$10 products?

Generally no. The \$2.00 flat small order fee on products below \$10 represents 20–25% of selling price at the \$8–\$10 range — before platform commission, transaction fee, or shipping. Most sub-\$10 products lose money on TikTok Shop unless they have extremely low COGS and no affiliate commission. The solution is almost always to bundle or reprice above \$10, where the small order fee disappears and all fees become percentage-based.

### What is a good TikTok Shop profit margin?

A healthy TikTok Shop profit margin is 25–40% after all fees including affiliate commission and shipping. Below 20%, the margin is too thin to absorb affiliate rate increases or shipping cost fluctuations. Above 40%, you have strong pricing power and room to increase affiliate commission to drive more creator promotion, which often yields better organic reach than keeping the margin and paying for ads.
