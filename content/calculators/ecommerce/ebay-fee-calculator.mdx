## What Your Results Mean

**Net Profit per Order** is what you actually pocket after eBay's final value fee, Managed Payments processing, promoted listings, your shipping cost, and your product cost.

**Profit Margin** is net profit as a percentage of your selling price — most healthy eBay businesses target 20–35%.

**Monthly Profit** projects your income at your entered order volume, useful for deciding whether a product is worth scaling.

## How to Use This Calculator

1. Enter your **Selling Price** — what the buyer pays for the item
2. Enter your **Product Cost** — what you paid to source or make it
3. Select your **Product Category** — this sets the final value fee rate
4. Enter **Shipping Charged to Buyer** (0 for free shipping) — eBay's FVF applies to item + shipping collected
5. Enter **Your Actual Shipping Cost** — your real postage + packaging expense
6. Optionally enter your **Promoted Listings Rate** (0 if not running ads)
7. Enter **Monthly Orders** to see your income projection

## eBay Fee Formula

<div className="formula-section">

eBay charges its final value fee on the **total amount the buyer pays** — including any shipping they pay. This is a commonly missed detail.

$$
\text{Final Value Fee} = \text{Buyer Total} \times \text{Category Rate}
$$

$$
\text{Buyer Total} = \text{Selling Price} + \text{Buyer Shipping}
$$

$$
\text{Payment Processing} = \text{Buyer Total} \times 2.9\% + \$0.30
$$

$$
\text{Net Profit} = \text{Selling Price} - \text{FVF} - \text{Processing} - \text{Promoted Fee} - \text{Actual Shipping} - \text{COGS}
$$

## Worked Example 1 — Home Goods (Free Shipping)

A seller lists a decorative ceramic vase for **$34.99** with free shipping. Product cost is $9.00.

| Fee | Calculation | Amount |
|-----|-------------|--------|
| Final Value Fee (13.25%) | $34.99 × 13.25% | $4.64 |
| Managed Payments | $34.99 × 2.9% + $0.30 | $1.31 |
| Actual Shipping Cost | (USPS Priority) | $8.50 |
| Product Cost | — | $9.00 |
| **Net Profit** | $34.99 − $4.64 − $1.31 − $8.50 − $9.00 | **$11.54** |
| **Profit Margin** | $11.54 / $34.99 | **33%** |

## Worked Example 2 — Electronics with Promoted Listings

A seller lists refurbished earbuds for **$49.99** with $5 buyer shipping, $4 actual shipping, and a 5% Promoted Listings rate. Product cost is $18.

| Fee | Calculation | Amount |
|-----|-------------|--------|
| Buyer Total | $49.99 + $5.00 | $54.99 |
| Final Value Fee (8.7%) | $54.99 × 8.7% | $4.78 |
| Managed Payments | $54.99 × 2.9% + $0.30 | $1.90 |
| Promoted Listings (5%) | $49.99 × 5% | $2.50 |
| Actual Shipping | — | $4.00 |
| Product Cost | — | $18.00 |
| **Net Profit** | $49.99 − $4.78 − $1.90 − $2.50 − $4.00 − $18.00 | **$18.81** |
| **Profit Margin** | $18.81 / $49.99 | **37.6%** |

Note: At 8.7%, electronics is one of eBay's lowest-rate categories — significantly better than the 13.25% default.

## Key Factors That Affect eBay Profit

**Category selection matters most.** The difference between 8.7% (electronics) and 15% (fashion under $100) is 6.3 percentage points — on a $50 sale, that's $3.15 of additional fee you can't recover.

**Free shipping isn't truly free.** Many sellers offer free shipping to win the algorithm, but eBay still charges its final value fee on the item price, not the buyer's total — so you absorb the shipping cost rather than losing FVF on it. Run the numbers both ways; sometimes charging shipping is more profitable.

**Promoted Listings Standard compounds costs.** At 5% on a $30 item, that's $1.50 per sale. If your promoted listings campaigns run at 5% and your natural profit margin is already thin at 20%, you're giving away 25% of your margin to ads.

**Insertion fees after 250 listings.** eBay allows 250 free listings per month. The 251st costs $0.35 each, and this adds up fast for high-SKU sellers — not captured in per-order calculators but relevant to monthly overhead.

**Return shipping policies.** eBay's "money-back guarantee" means buyers can return items for any reason. Sellers absorbing return shipping on $8–$12 postage costs can easily wipe out 2–3 weeks of profit on a slow SKU.

## Assumptions & Limitations

- Rates are for **eBay US private sellers** (2025). Business accounts and store subscribers may have different rates.
- Final value fee applies to total buyer-paid amount including buyer shipping — this calculator models that correctly.
- eBay may charge a final value fee on successfully resolved disputes or returns at their discretion.
- Promoted Listings Standard fee applies only when a sale results from the promoted placement.
- Store subscribers (Basic, Premium, Anchor) receive reduced fees on certain categories — this calculator does not model store subscription discounts.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**What is eBay's final value fee?**
eBay's final value fee is a percentage of the total buyer-paid amount (item price + shipping charged) collected when a sale completes. The rate ranges from 8.7% for electronics to 15% for fashion items under $100, with most categories at 13.25%. eBay charges the fee on both the item price and any shipping amount the buyer pays.

**Does eBay charge fees on shipping?**
Yes. eBay's final value fee applies to the total buyer-paid amount, which includes any shipping fee collected from the buyer. If you charge $5 for shipping and your item sells for $30, eBay charges 13.25% of $35 = $4.64, not $3.98. Offering free shipping shifts the shipping cost to you but avoids eBay taking a cut of it.

**What is Managed Payments and how much does it cost?**
eBay Managed Payments replaced PayPal as eBay's payment processor. The standard rate is 2.9% of the total buyer amount + $0.30 per order. For most sellers, Managed Payments costs slightly less than the old PayPal arrangement (which was 2.9% + $0.30 on top of eBay fees separately).

**How do Promoted Listings Standard fees work?**
Promoted Listings Standard is a pay-per-sale ad model — you set an ad rate (typically 2–10%) and only pay when your item sells through the promoted placement. The fee is calculated as your chosen rate × the item's selling price. You are not charged if the item sells organically without the buyer clicking the promoted listing.
