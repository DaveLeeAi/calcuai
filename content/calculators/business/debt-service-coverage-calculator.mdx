## What Your Results Mean

**Debt Service Coverage Ratio (DSCR)** is the single most important metric commercial lenders use to evaluate loan applications. It measures how many times your net operating income covers your required debt payments. A DSCR of 1.50x means you earn 50% more than needed to cover debt — a comfortable margin of safety. A DSCR of 1.0x means every dollar of NOI goes to debt payments with nothing left over. Below 1.0x, you cannot cover your debt from operations.

**Excess Cash Flow** is the dollar amount remaining after subtracting total debt service from NOI. Positive excess cash flow means you have money for reserves, improvements, distributions, or unexpected expenses. Negative excess cash flow means you must supplement income from other sources to make debt payments — a situation lenders will not finance.

**Maximum Affordable Debt Service** calculates the largest annual payment you could make while maintaining the lender's required DSCR. This is useful for determining how much you can borrow: if a lender requires a 1.25x DSCR and your NOI is \$150,000, your maximum annual debt service is \$120,000.

**Qualification Status** tells you whether you meet the lender's minimum DSCR requirement. "Qualifies" means your DSCR meets or exceeds the requirement. "Borderline" means you cover your debt but fall short of the required ratio. "Does Not Qualify" means your NOI cannot cover the debt payments at all.

## How to Use This Calculator

1. **Enter net operating income (NOI)** — This is your annual revenue minus operating expenses (property taxes, insurance, maintenance, management fees, vacancy allowance). Do NOT subtract debt payments — the purpose of DSCR is to measure NOI relative to debt.
2. **Enter annual debt service** — The total annual principal plus interest payments on the loan you are evaluating. For a \$1,000,000 loan at 7% over 25 years, annual debt service is approximately \$84,660.
3. **Enter the required DSCR** — Ask your lender for their minimum requirement. Typical ranges: SBA loans 1.15-1.25x, conventional commercial 1.20-1.35x, multifamily 1.20-1.25x, construction loans 1.30-1.50x.
4. **Read your results** — If the status says "Qualifies," you meet the lender's minimum. If "Borderline," you may need to increase your down payment (reducing loan amount and debt service) or demonstrate additional income sources.

## The Formula

<div className="formula-section">

**Debt Service Coverage Ratio:**

$$
\text{DSCR} = \frac{\text{Net Operating Income}}{\text{Total Annual Debt Service}}
$$

**Excess Cash Flow:**

$$
\text{Excess Cash Flow} = \text{NOI} - \text{Total Debt Service}
$$

**Maximum Affordable Debt Service:**

$$
\text{Max Payment} = \frac{\text{NOI}}{\text{Required DSCR}}
$$

Where:
- **Net Operating Income (NOI)** = gross revenue minus operating expenses (excluding debt payments and depreciation)
- **Total Annual Debt Service** = total annual principal payments + total annual interest payments
- **Required DSCR** = minimum ratio demanded by the lender (typically 1.20-1.50x)

*Source: Federal Reserve — "Commercial Real Estate Lending Standards" (SR 15-17); FDIC risk management guidance. DSCR is the primary underwriting metric for commercial real estate and SBA lending.*

## Worked Examples

### Example 1: Multifamily Apartment Building

An investor is purchasing a 20-unit apartment building generating \$18,000/month in gross rental income (\$216,000/year). After vacancy (5%), property taxes (\$18,000), insurance (\$8,000), maintenance (\$12,000), and management (8% of collected rent = \$16,416), the NOI is \$144,984. The proposed loan is \$1,200,000 at 6.75% over 25 years with annual debt service of \$100,500. The lender requires a 1.25x DSCR.

**DSCR = \$144,984 / \$100,500 = 1.44x**

- Excess Cash Flow = \$144,984 - \$100,500 = **\$44,484/year**
- Max Debt Service at 1.25x = \$144,984 / 1.25 = **\$115,987/year**
- Qualification: **Qualifies** (1.44x exceeds 1.25x requirement)

*Interpretation:* With a 1.44x DSCR, this deal has a 15% buffer above the lender's 1.25x minimum. The \$44,484 excess cash flow covers approximately 5.3 months of debt payments — a healthy reserve. The investor could theoretically borrow up to the point where debt service reaches \$115,987/year and still meet the requirement.

### Example 2: Retail Strip Mall — Borderline

A strip mall generates \$95,000 in NOI after all operating expenses. The existing mortgage requires \$82,000 in annual debt service. The owner wants to refinance at a lender requiring 1.25x DSCR.

**DSCR = \$95,000 / \$82,000 = 1.16x**

- Excess Cash Flow = \$95,000 - \$82,000 = **\$13,000/year**
- Max Debt Service at 1.25x = \$95,000 / 1.25 = **\$76,000/year**
- Qualification: **Borderline** (1.16x is above 1.0 but below 1.25x)

*Interpretation:* This property covers its debt but fails the 1.25x test. The lender may decline or offer less favorable terms. To qualify, the owner has three options: (1) increase NOI by \$7,500/year through rent increases or expense reduction (pushing DSCR to 1.25x), (2) put more equity down to reduce the loan amount until debt service drops to \$76,000 or less, or (3) find a lender with a lower DSCR requirement (some portfolio lenders accept 1.15x).

## Key Factors That Affect Your Results

1. **NOI Calculation Is Everything** — Lenders verify NOI independently. They will use their own vacancy and expense assumptions, which are often more conservative than yours. If you assume 3% vacancy and the lender underwrites at 8%, your DSCR drops significantly. Always calculate DSCR using the lender's assumptions, not your optimistic projections.

2. **Interest Rate Changes Hit DSCR Hard** — A \$1,000,000 loan at 5% has annual debt service of about \$70,151 (25-year amortization). At 7%, it jumps to \$84,660. That 2% rate increase reduces DSCR by roughly 17% on the same NOI. In rising rate environments, properties that qualified at 5% may fail at 7%.

3. **Amortization Period Matters** — A 30-year amortization produces lower annual payments than a 20-year amortization on the same principal and rate. Some lenders offer interest-only periods for the first 1-3 years, which maximizes DSCR during the initial period but increases payments later.

4. **Required DSCR Varies by Property Type** — Hotels and restaurants (volatile income) require 1.40-1.60x. Multifamily apartments (stable income) require 1.20-1.25x. Office and retail (moderate risk) require 1.25-1.35x. Ask your specific lender — requirements also vary by borrower experience and loan-to-value ratio.

## Assumptions & Limitations

- NOI should be calculated using trailing 12-month actual income and expenses, not projections — lenders discount projections heavily
- Total debt service should include all loans secured by the property, not just the new loan being underwritten
- Does not account for personal income or guarantees that a lender may consider as additional repayment sources
- The required DSCR is a minimum — many lenders look for a margin above their minimum before approving
- Property taxes and insurance can increase over the loan term, reducing NOI and DSCR after closing
- Does not calculate the loan amount — use this calculator alongside a loan amortization calculator to determine what principal amount produces the debt service you entered

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What DSCR do most commercial lenders require?

Most conventional commercial real estate lenders require a minimum DSCR of 1.20x to 1.35x, with 1.25x being the most common threshold. SBA 504 and 7(a) loans typically require 1.15x to 1.25x. CMBS (commercial mortgage-backed securities) lenders generally require 1.25x to 1.30x. Construction and development loans often require 1.30x to 1.50x due to higher risk. These minimums represent the floor — stronger DSCRs earn better rates, higher leverage, and faster approvals.

### How can I improve my DSCR to qualify for a loan?

The fastest way to improve DSCR is to reduce debt service by increasing your down payment, which lowers the loan amount and monthly payment. If you increase a down payment from 20% to 30% on a \$1,000,000 property, you reduce the loan from \$800,000 to \$700,000 — cutting annual debt service by roughly 12.5% and boosting your DSCR proportionally. On the income side, raising rents, reducing vacancy, or cutting operating expenses all increase NOI. Even small changes compound: a 5% rent increase combined with a 2% expense reduction can push a borderline DSCR from 1.18x to 1.30x.

### What is the difference between DSCR and debt-to-income ratio?

DSCR is used for commercial and investment property lending, measuring property-level net operating income against property-level debt payments. Debt-to-income (DTI) ratio is used for personal and residential lending, measuring a borrower's total personal income against total personal debt obligations. A borrower can have an excellent personal DTI (low personal debt relative to salary) but a poor DSCR on an investment property (the property does not generate enough income to cover its mortgage). Lenders evaluate both for investment property loans.
