## What Your Results Mean

**Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC)** is the total cost of marketing and sales divided by the number of new customers acquired during the same period. It answers the fundamental question: how much does it cost to get one new customer?

**CAC Payback Period** tells you how many months of revenue from a new customer it takes to recover the cost of acquiring them. A payback under 12 months is generally considered healthy for SaaS businesses; under 6 months is excellent. For e-commerce, payback on the first purchase is the ideal target.

**Cost Breakdown** shows how your acquisition spend splits between marketing and sales, helping you identify which channel deserves more or less investment.

## How to Use This Calculator

1. **Enter Marketing Spend** — total marketing costs for the period (ad spend, content creation, tools, agency fees).
2. **Enter Sales Team Cost** — total sales department costs (salaries, commissions, tools, travel).
3. **Enter New Customers** — the number of net-new customers acquired in the same period. Do not count renewals or reactivations.
4. **Enter Monthly Revenue per Customer** — the average monthly revenue from a newly acquired customer, used to calculate payback period.

{/* Section 8: The Formula */}
## The Formula

<div className="formula-section">

$$
\text{CAC} = \frac{\text{Marketing Spend} + \text{Sales Spend}}{\text{New Customers Acquired}}
$$

$$
\text{CAC Payback (months)} = \frac{\text{CAC}}{\text{Monthly Revenue per Customer}}
$$

Where:
- **Marketing Spend** = all marketing costs during the period
- **Sales Spend** = all sales team costs during the period
- **New Customers Acquired** = net-new customers gained during the same period
- **Monthly Revenue per Customer** = average monthly revenue from a single customer

*Source: SaaS Metrics 2.0 — David Skok, Matrix Partners (2023). CAC methodology widely adopted in venture capital and SaaS valuation.*

</div>

## Worked Example

A B2B SaaS company spends \$25,000 on content marketing, paid search, and events in Q1. The sales team costs \$18,000 in salary and commission for the same quarter. They close 15 new customers at an average plan of \$199/month.

**CAC = (\$25,000 + \$18,000) / 15 = \$2,866.67**

- Marketing share = \$25,000 / \$43,000 = **58.1%**
- Sales share = \$18,000 / \$43,000 = **41.9%**
- CAC Payback = \$2,866.67 / \$199 = **14.4 months**

*Interpretation:* A 14.4-month payback exceeds the 12-month benchmark for healthy SaaS economics. The company should either increase the contract value (upsell to annual plans), reduce churn to extend customer lifetime, or find lower-cost acquisition channels. Paired with a [CLV calculator](/calculators/business/customer-lifetime-value-calculator), this data reveals whether the unit economics sustain growth.

## Key Factors That Affect Your CAC

1. **Channel Mix** — Organic channels (SEO, referrals, word-of-mouth) have near-zero marginal CAC, while paid channels (Google Ads, Facebook Ads) have directly measurable costs. A business with 60% organic traffic typically has 40-60% lower blended CAC than one that relies entirely on paid acquisition.

2. **Sales Cycle Length** — Longer sales cycles increase CAC because sales staff costs accumulate. Enterprise SaaS with 6-month sales cycles typically has 3-5x higher CAC than self-serve products with same-day signups.

3. **Market Maturity** — CAC increases as markets become more competitive. Early entrants in a new category may see CAC of \$50-100; in a saturated market, the same customer could cost \$500-1,000.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What is a good CAC for my industry?

CAC varies widely by industry and business model. According to First Page Sage (2024): SaaS averages \$205-\$650 depending on deal size, e-commerce averages \$45-\$90, financial services \$200-\$600, and education \$55-\$130. More important than the absolute number is the CAC:LTV ratio — aim for LTV that is at least 3x your CAC regardless of industry.

### How do I lower my CAC?

The three highest-impact strategies are: (1) investing in organic acquisition channels like SEO and referral programs, which reduce marginal CAC to near zero over time; (2) improving conversion rates at each funnel stage — doubling your website conversion rate from 2% to 4% halves your effective CAC; and (3) shortening the sales cycle through better qualification, self-serve demos, and streamlined onboarding.

### Should I include salaries in my CAC calculation?

Yes. A fully loaded CAC includes all costs attributable to customer acquisition: marketing team salaries, sales team salaries and commissions, ad spend, tools and software, agency fees, event costs, and content production. Excluding salaries significantly understates true CAC — David Skok's framework (SaaS Metrics 2.0) explicitly includes fully loaded costs for accurate unit economics.
